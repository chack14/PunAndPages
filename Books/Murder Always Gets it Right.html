Jeff McDaniel's poem "Murder Always Gets It Right" delves into the inevitability and precision of violence and loss, portraying murder as a force that is both inescapable and exacting in its execution. The poem personifies murder, highlighting its meticulous nature and its capacity to irrevocably alter lives while leaving a haunting emptiness in its wake. Through stark imagery and a tone both chilling and reflective, McDaniel explores the permanence of death and its impact on those left behind. The poem serves as a meditation on mortality and the fragility of life, emphasizing the cold certainty with which murder fulfills its grim purpose.
